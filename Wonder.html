Gods and Mortals

This is the definitive Wonder Woman origin story written by George Pérez, Greg Potter and Len Wein, and begins Pérez's renowned 62-issue run on the title. The story establishes the origins of Diana and the Amazons, and follows Diana's venture from Paradise Island to Man's World, where she faces the villain Ares.

Challenge of the Gods

The second storyline by Pérez and Wein, introduces the villain Cheetah before delving into a story in which Wonder Woman is set a challenge by the Gods of Olympus to venture into the caves beneath Themyscira.

War of the Gods

A crossover event written by Pérez which centers largely on Wonder Woman and one of her most famous villains, Circe.

JLA: A League of One

A graphic novel written and illustrated by Christopher Moeller and starring Wonder Woman. An Amazon oracle tells Diana of a prophecy she has seen: the Justice League will fight an evil dragon and lose their lives in the process. Wonder Woman attempts to save her teammates' lives and face the dragon alone.

Paradise Island Lost

A graphic novel written and illustrated by Christopher Moeller and starring Wonder Woman. An Amazon oracle tells Diana of a prophecy she has seen: the Justice League will fight an evil dragon and lose their lives in the process. Wonder Woman attempts to save her teammates' lives and face the dragon alone.

Spirit of Truth

A graphic novel written by Paul Dini and Alex Ross and featuring art by Ross. The story deals with the world's perception of Wonder Woman.

The Hiketeia

A graphic novel written by Greg Rucka, the first of his work with the character. Wonder Woman finds herself at odds with Batman when she partakes in an ancient Greek ritual called the Hiketeia to protect a young woman whom Batman is hunting for murder.

Down to Earth

Begins Rucka's acclaimed arc of Wonder Woman. Diana, now a diplomat working with the United Nations, faces public backlash and protests after publishing a book, and clashes with both classic and new villains.

Stoned

Rucka's run continues with an action-packed story in which Wonder Woman must battle the resurrected Gorgon Medusa, making great sacrifices in the process.
Wonder Woman (Volume 2) #206-210

Superman: Sacrifice

Superman is mind controlled by Maxwell Lord, and Wonder Woman must stop him from wreaking havoc even if it means taking drastic measures. This story leads into the massive crossover event Infinite Crisis. Superman (Volume 2) #219, Action Comics #829, Adventures of Superman #642-643, Wonder Woman (Volume 2) #219

Who is Wonder Woman?

One Year Later, Diana has abandoned the role of Wonder Woman, with her apprentice Donna Troy taking over the mantle. Diana is working for the Department of Metahuman Affairs as secret agent Diana Prince, but is forced to intervene when Circe establishes a team of all of Wonder Woman's greatest foes. Wonder Woman (Volume 3) #1-4, Annual 2007.
